Oil Majors Long Thesis Section 1: The Hormuz Crisis — What Actually Happened

The Hormuz Crisis — What Actually Happened

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel conducted joint military strikes on Iran, including the killing of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei. Iran retaliated immediately — launching missile and drone attacks on US military bases, Israeli territory, and Gulf states. The IRGC issued warnings prohibiting vessel passage through the Strait of Hormuz, and within 48 hours tanker traffic had dropped approximately 70%, with over 150 ships anchoring outside the strait. By March 2, an IRGC commander officially confirmed the strait was closed and threatened to set ablaze any vessel attempting to transit.

This is not a geopolitical risk premium. This is physical supply destruction in real time. The strait carries approximately 20 million barrels of oil per day — roughly 20% of global seaborne oil trade and 20% of global LNG supply. Major container shipping companies including Maersk, CMA CGM, and Hapag-Lloyd have suspended all transits. Insurance withdrawal has done the work that a physical blockade has not — the outcome for cargo flow is the same.

They are vertically integrated energy majors with refining, chemicals, LNG, and upstream production — meaning they benefit from both the oil price spike and the supply chain disruption simultaneously. Their balance sheets were rebuilt post-2020 specifically to withstand low oil environments, which means at $80+ WTI they are generating exceptional free cash flow.

Position Sizing & Risk Management

Leverage amplifies conviction — it also amplifies mistakes. The disciplined framework below is designed to keep the thesis alive through the inevitable false-signal volatility without being shaken out before the thesis resolves.

Core Sizing Rule
At 1:3 leverage: Size the total leveraged exposure so that a −33% move in XOM/CVX from your average entry does not wipe more than 15–20% of total portfolio. This means the underlying equity allocation before leverage should be 5–7% of total savings.

At 1:5 leverage: No more than 2–3% of total portfolio in underlying before leverage. The gap risk on a geopolitical surprise (ceasefire announced overnight) is real — 1:5 on a 5% position means a −20% gap wipes the entire allocation.
