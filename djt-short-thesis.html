The Thesis in One Paragraph

Trump Media & Technology Group is a company with no viable operating business, a deteriorating financial structure, and a stock price that has declined 62.9% over twelve months and 83% from its all-time high — yet remains valued at $2.84 billion. The bull case rests entirely on the $2.5 billion balance sheet and the political brand premium. Both are eroding. Bitcoin — now the primary asset — has fallen 22% year-to-date. The $6 billion TAE fusion merger is a speculative bet on technology that has never produced commercial-scale energy anywhere in the world. The Truth Social spinoff, if completed, separates the only brand-attached asset from the remaining entity. What is left is a holding company with declining crypto assets, a social platform with 359,000 daily active users, and an Altman Z-Score of 2.57 — firmly in the distress zone.

This report presents six independent lines of evidence for a continued and potentially terminal decline in DJT's equity value, along with a structured put options framework for expressing the thesis with defined risk and asymmetric reward.

This is not a pre-revenue startup — it is a post-launch platform that has been operating for four years and cannot grow.

Evidence II — The Bitcoin Treasury Is Bleeding

In May 2025, management made a catastrophic strategic pivot: raising $2.5 billion to transform DJT into a Bitcoin treasury company, issuing 55.8 million new shares and $1 billion in zero-interest convertible notes to fund the purchase. This diluted every existing shareholder by approximately 25% in a single transaction. The stated rationale — to tie DJT's stock price to Bitcoin rather than Truth Social's performance — has since backfired dramatically.

Bitcoin has fallen from $88,738 on January 1, 2026 to approximately $68,949 today — a decline of over 22% year-to-date. The company recorded $582 million in non-cash digital asset markdowns in FY2025, the primary driver of the $712 million net loss. Separately, DJT purchased 684.4 million CRO (Cronos) tokens at 15.3 cents per token — those same tokens now trade at approximately 7.8 cents, creating an unrealized loss of over $51 million. The planned CRO Strategy Company hasn't materialized due to the price collapse.

Evidence IV — The TAE Merger Is a Speculative Fantasy

On December 18, 2025, DJT announced a $6 billion all-stock merger with TAE Technologies — a nuclear fusion startup founded in 1998 that has been pursuing commercial fusion for 27 years without success. The stated goal is to create a "utility-scale fusion plant" by 2026 onwards. No fusion company has ever produced net energy at commercial scale anywhere in the world. The National Ignition Facility's landmark 2022 experiment produced 3.15 megajoules from 2.05 megajoules of laser energy — a breakthrough in physics, but still years from a commercially viable plant.

▼ Why the TAE Merger Doesn't Save DJT
1. No proven commercial fusion technology exists anywhere. TAE has been operating since 1998 — 28 years — without reaching commercial viability.

2. The deal is all-stock. DJT shareholders receive 50% of a combined entity whose primary asset is speculative IP. No cash, no earnings, no revenue.

3. The market has already cooled. After an initial bump on Dec 18, DJT resumed its downtrend. The stock is lower today than before the announcement.

4. Truth Social spinoff separates the brand. If the spinoff proceeds before the merger closes, the entity merging with TAE won't even contain Truth Social — meaning DJT holders get a media spinco AND a fusion company with zero revenue between them.

5. Fusion will need massive government support. Given DJT's conflicts of interest with the current administration, regulatory approvals create both risk and optics concerns.

The Put Options Framework: Buying the Decline

With 30-day implied volatility at 69.68%, put options on DJT are expensive in percentage terms — but the key insight is that on a $10 stock, the absolute premium per contract is very low. A $10 put costs approximately $1.70–$2.00. The thesis requires a move to $5–7 in the bull-for-bears scenario or toward $2–3 in the terminal scenario — both multiples of any put premium at current strikes.

Unlike the XOM/CVX call thesis which requires a positive event, the DJT put thesis is event-agnostic in the negative direction: TAE merger failure, continued Bitcoin decline, another dilutive issuance, or simply continued revenue stagnation — any of these independently is sufficient to drive further price deterioration. The bear thesis does not need a single catalyst. It needs time and the absence of rescue.
